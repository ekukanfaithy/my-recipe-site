Nigerian Smoky Jellof Recipe

A classic West African one-pot dish made with long-grain rice, tomatoes, peppers, and spices, perfectly cooked to be flavorful, vibrant, and irresistible.

Recipe Details

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook time: 40 minutes

Servings: 4-6 people

Difficulty: Medium

Ingredient List

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 4 large tomatoes, blended
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ cup vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Prep your ingredients: Blend the tomatoes and red bell pepper until smooth. Chop the onion.
  2. In a large pot, heat ½ cup vegetable oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the chopped onions and fry for about 2 minutes.
  4. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes to remove the raw taste.
  5. Pour in the blended tomatoes and peppers, stir, and let it cook for 10 minutes until the sauce thickens and the oil begins to separate.
  6. Add thyme, curry powder, bay leaves, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
  7. Rinse the rice until the water runs clear, then add it to the pot.
  8. Pour in the chicken or vegetable stock, ensuring the liquid covers the rice.
  9. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for 20–25 minutes until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.

Tip

For an aesthetically cooked rice, use Basmatti rice.
